# Frontier Intelligence Capital — Investment Thesis 2026

## One-line Thesis

AI will structurally increase the economic power of capital, compute, platforms, and scalable technology assets. Public markets offer liquid exposure to this transition.

## Strategic Focus

### 1. AI Infrastructure

- Semiconductors
- Accelerators / GPUs
- Networking
- Data centers
- Power and cooling infrastructure
- Cloud compute platforms

### 2. Platform Companies

Companies with:

- massive distribution
- proprietary data
- cloud ecosystems
- enterprise relationships
- developer ecosystems
- pricing power

### 3. Software Productivity

AI-native software may expand margins and productivity across:

- engineering
- sales
- customer support
- workflow automation
- enterprise operations

### 4. Index-level Exposure

QQQ / Nasdaq-100 exposure remains a core expression of the thesis because it captures diversified participation in large-cap technology, AI infrastructure, cloud, software, and platform companies.

## Strategy Framework

### Core Instruments

- QQQ / Nasdaq-100 exposure
- Deep ITM LEAPS calls
- QLD / leveraged ETF exposure where appropriate
- QQQ spot allocation as lower-risk base layer

### LEAPS Philosophy

Deep in-the-money LEAPS provide:

- high delta exposure
- lower theta decay than OTM options
- defined capital outlay
- leveraged participation in long-term technology upside

Preferred structure:

- approximately 1.5 years to expiry at entry
- around 0.75 delta
- roll when remaining duration approaches ~9 months

### Profit Handling

When LEAPS generate significant gains, part of the profit should be transferred into lower-risk exposure:

- QQQ spot
- QLD
- cash / short-duration reserves if risk conditions require

This keeps upside participation while reducing single-instrument fragility.

## Risk Philosophy

The strategy is bullish on technology, but not blind to risk.

Key risks:

- valuation compression
- AI capex disappointment
- interest rate repricing
- liquidity shocks
- geopolitical risk
- option theta and IV risk
- concentration risk
- forced selling during drawdowns

Risk controls:

- manage duration and roll windows
- avoid excessive near-expiry option exposure
- monitor delta and portfolio concentration
- keep a reserve buffer for tail events
- avoid emotional trading during volatility spikes

## What We Are Not

Frontier Intelligence Capital is not:

- a short-term trading shop
- a crypto speculation vehicle
- a hype-driven AI startup picker
- a market-timing newsletter

The approach is long-term, technology-focused, and ownership-oriented.

## Summary

AI changes who earns, who owns, and who compounds.

Frontier Intelligence Capital exists to participate in that transition through disciplined public market investing.
