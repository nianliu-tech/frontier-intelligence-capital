AI will structurally increase the economic power of capital, compute, platforms, and scalable technology assets. Public markets offer liquid exposure to this transition.

Investment Philosophy

Frontier Intelligence Capital combines long-term ownership, valuation discipline, portfolio resilience, and probabilistic thinking. The approach is influenced by several enduring schools of investment thought, but is adapted for public markets in the age of artificial intelligence.

Margin of Safety

From Benjamin Graham, the strategy emphasizes downside protection, valuation awareness, and avoiding permanent capital impairment. Even when the portfolio is growth-oriented, every position should be evaluated against drawdown risk, duration risk, and liquidity risk.

Quality and Long-term Compounding

From Charlie Munger and Philip Fisher, the strategy favors businesses and indexes connected to durable competitive advantages: scale, distribution, data, ecosystem control, pricing power, and high reinvestment potential.

Market Efficiency and Humility

From the tradition of A Random Walk Down Wall Street, the framework assumes markets are difficult to outguess in the short term. Index-level exposure remains important because broad ownership of productive assets can be more reliable than repeated short-term prediction.

Risk Parity and Portfolio Balance

The portfolio is not managed solely by capital allocation, but by risk contribution. Cash reserves, lower-volatility exposure, and position sizing are used to prevent any single instrument, theme, or volatility regime from dominating the portfolio.

Talebian Robustness

From Nassim Nicholas Taleb, the strategy respects fat tails, uncertainty, and the danger of hidden fragility. Defined-risk instruments, liquidity buffers, and optionality are preferred over structures that can force selling at the worst possible time.

AI-era Adaptation

The core belief is that AI may increase the economic power of capital, compute, platforms, and scalable technology assets. The portfolio therefore seeks exposure to that transition while maintaining explicit controls around concentration, leverage, duration, and liquidity.

Strategy Framework

Core Instruments

  • QQQ / Nasdaq-100 exposure
  • Deep ITM LEAPS calls
  • QLD / leveraged ETF exposure where appropriate
  • QQQ spot allocation as lower-risk base layer

LEAPS Philosophy

Deep in-the-money LEAPS provide high delta exposure, lower theta decay than OTM options, defined capital outlay, and leveraged participation in long-term technology upside.

Preferred structure: approximately 1.5 years to expiry at entry, around 0.75 delta, with rolls when remaining duration approaches roughly nine months.

Profit Handling

When LEAPS generate significant gains, part of the profit should be transferred into lower-risk exposure such as QQQ spot, QLD, or cash / short-duration reserves if risk conditions require.

This keeps upside participation while reducing single-instrument fragility.

Risk Philosophy

The strategy is bullish on technology, but not blind to risk.

Key risks include valuation compression, AI capex disappointment, interest rate repricing, liquidity shocks, geopolitical risk, option theta and IV risk, concentration risk, and forced selling during drawdowns.

Risk controls include managing duration and roll windows, avoiding excessive near-expiry option exposure, monitoring delta and portfolio concentration, keeping a reserve buffer for tail events, and avoiding emotional trading during volatility spikes.

What We Are Not

Frontier Intelligence Capital is not a short-term trading shop, a crypto speculation vehicle, a hype-driven AI startup picker, or a market-timing newsletter.

The approach is long-term, technology-focused, and ownership-oriented.
